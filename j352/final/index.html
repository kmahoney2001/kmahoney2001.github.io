The Gallery . . .








Today, viewing art as a student often comes with a price. From attending galleries to simply going to the movies, prices of travel and viewing art seem unfeasible for the average college student. Situated within the walls of the Parren J. Mitchell Art and Sociology building at the University of Maryland lies a solution to the problem of accessing free art, the University of Maryland Art Gallery.

The University of Maryland Art Gallery began in 1966 with the collection of prints and drawings from Martin W. Brown. It started out in the Tawes Fine Arts Center and moved into the Art-Sociology Building in 1976.

Throughout the years at the University of Maryland there have been endless exhibits covering all different forms of art. While the gallery has almost 4500 objects, they often showcase work by outside artists.

The Recent . . .





Recent exhibitions include Emilio Sánchez x Lost Sock Roasters exhibit which ran from June 20 to October 7, 2022. The University of Maryland worked alongside Lost Sock Roasters from DC to present Cuban artist Emilio Sánchez. With a focus on architectural prints with pops of bright hues, Sánchez ties in the color palette of traditional Caribbean architecture to express origins and place.

Earlier in the year, the University of Maryland’s Art Gallery hosted artist Daniela Libertad’s first ever exhibition on United States soil, Daniela Libertad: Yo regreso a las acciones sencillas. Libertad is a Mexico City-born artist residing in Germany. She is a multidisciplinary artist who uses drawings, sculptures, photographs and videos. In this exhibit she highlights the connection between body and object while embodying storytelling traditions

There are also MFA shows to showcase graduate students' work. This includes the one that occurred this past spring with Alyssa Imes, Elizabeth Katt and Martin Gonzales.

The Virtual . . .

With the semester coming to a close, many of the in-person exhibits are ending, including Video in the Atrium (VITA): Cristina Lucas, which ends December 30 and Christopher Bartel: The Skinwalker Ranch Portfolio which ends December 31 leaving students only a few days left to visit them in-person. However, while many students leave before the holidays, the art gallery provides online viewings of some exhibitions while they are running. Students are free to leave campus and retreat home with the ability to view current exhibits from their hometown.

Throughout the time the art gallery has been open, there are multiple exhibitions that return frequently with new pieces and artists to see. One of the frequent returners to the gallery is the faculty exhibition.







The why . . .




Whether it’s a returning show or a never-before-seen exhibition, the University of Maryland’s Art Gallery prioritizes gathering and displaying art from all different artists, cultures and methods to educate the University of Maryland community. Stop into the art gallery any weekday to and prepare to envelope yourself in art from all around the world . . . for free.
